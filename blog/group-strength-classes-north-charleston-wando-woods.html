What Happens in Each Session

Every session follows a proven structure built by a Certified Strength & Conditioning Specialist (CSCS). The workout adapts to your track — but the framework is always the same: warm up right, move athletically, build strength.

  • Floor Warm-Up & Stretching — Mobility and joint prep to reduce injury risk and prime your body for work.
  • Dynamic Movements — Athletic movement patterns building coordination, agility, and body control.
  • Ladder Drills & Medicine Ball Power Circuit — Speed, reactive agility, and explosive power. Especially targeted in the youth sprint/vertical track.
  • Sprint Work & Vertical Jump Training — (Youth track) Acceleration mechanics, max velocity work, and plyometric jump training with weekly speed & power testing.
  • Strength & Fitness Capacity Circuit — Coach-programmed strength work targeting major muscle groups. Scaled to your level.

Why Park & Home Workouts Get You Better Results

Training outdoors or at home isn't a compromise — for most people, it's actually the faster path to their goals. A crowded commercial gym full of machines looks impressive, but it's not what builds a strong, capable, athletic body. Open space and your own bodyweight are.

  • Consistency is what actually drives results — The best program is the one you keep showing up to. When training is five minutes from your door instead of a 30-minute round trip to a packed gym, you train more often. More sessions completed beats a "perfect" gym you skip.
  • Real, functional strength — Uneven grass, open ground, and bodyweight movement force your stabilizers, core, and joints to work the way they do in real life and in sport. Machines lock you into a fixed path; the outdoors trains strength that actually carries over.
  • Space to move athletically — Sprinting, jumping, throwing, and crawling need room most gyms don't have. A park gives you the space to develop true speed and power — the qualities that change how a youth athlete performs and how an adult moves and ages.
  • Mastering the fundamentals — Minimal equipment removes the distractions and forces quality reps of the movements that build the most strength: squats, hinges, pushes, pulls, and carries. Fundamentals done well outperform fancy machines done lazily.
  • The outdoor advantage — Fresh air, natural light, and training under the sky lower stress, lift mood, and make hard work feel sustainable. People who enjoy their training stick with it — and adherence is the single biggest predictor of long-term results.
  • Accountability close to home — Training with 3–5 people you know, in your own neighborhood, builds a routine and a crew that keeps everyone coming back. Community is a performance enhancer.
"The gym doesn't make the athlete — the work does. Give me an open field, a few people who want to get better, and a smart plan, and we'll out-train a fancy facility every time."
— Coach Adam Oliver, CSCS

None of this means you need to give up the gym forever — it means you don't have to wait for one to start making progress. Park and home sessions remove every excuse between you and your goals: no membership, no commute, no crowds, no waiting on equipment. Just coaching, your group, and the work.
