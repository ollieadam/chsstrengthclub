The Background

Trained at the World's Best

Mike Boyle Strength & Conditioning (MBSC) in Boston is one of the most respected performance training facilities in the world. It's where professional athletes, D1 college athletes, and Olympic competitors train — and where the methods that define modern strength and conditioning were developed.

Coach Adam Oliver holds a Bachelor's degree in Physical Education & Exercise Science from Norwich University and trained directly at MBSC alongside coaches who work with professional and D1 athletes every day. That experience — watching how elite athletes are actually built, learning the systems behind the results — is the foundation of every training program at Charleston Strength Club.

What MBSC Training Means for Your Athlete

The programming your athlete receives isn't adapted from a YouTube video or a generic app. It's built on the same principles used to develop professional hockey players, D1 football athletes, and Olympic competitors. Functional movement, progressive overload, speed development, and injury prevention — applied specifically to youth and high school athletes in North Charleston.

"The gym doesn't make the athlete. The system does. And you can run that system anywhere — a park, an empty room, a field. The equipment is secondary. The knowledge is everything." — Coach Adam Oliver, CSCS · Former SC Stingrays S&C Coach

Programs

What We Train

Every program is built around what actually moves the needle for athletes — not what looks impressive on social media. Small groups of 3–5 athletes get individualized coaching inside a team environment.

01
Sprint Speed & Acceleration

Mechanics-first sprint development. We fix how your athlete runs before we ask them to run faster. Acceleration, max velocity, and change of direction — tested and tracked weekly.

02
Vertical Jump & Explosive Power

Plyometric progressions and strength work that translate to the court and field. Jump height, reactive strength, and landing mechanics — all measured so you can see the progress.

03
Strength & Injury Prevention

Building the athletic base that keeps athletes healthy through long seasons. FMS-based movement screening, progressive strength programming, and sport-specific conditioning.

04
Sport-Specific Conditioning

Aerobic and anaerobic capacity built for your sport's demands. Not generic cardio — energy system development that matches what your athlete actually needs on game day.

The Environment

Elite Training Anywhere

The Park IS the Gym

One of the biggest lessons from MBSC: the best coaches don't need a $5 million facility to produce elite results. They need a system, a plan, and the ability to execute it wherever they are.

Coach Adam has trained professional hockey players in conventional gym settings and turned a public park in North Charleston into a high-performance training environment. The principles don't change. The sprint mechanics are the same. The jump progressions are the same. The results are the same.

When the community center is unavailable, the session moves. We train at local parks, open fields, or your home. No excuses. No cancelled sessions.
